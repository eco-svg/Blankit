📏 How to measure each field

Every field below is a circumference — wrap the tape all the way around, snug but not tight — except Height, which is your standing height floor-to-head. Stand relaxed, arms at your sides, tape horizontal (level with the floor) at each spot.

  • Height — the one length measurement: barefoot, standing straight, floor to the top of your head.
  • Weight — bathroom scale, not tape.
  • Neck — all the way around, just below the Adam's apple, head facing forward.
  • Shoulders — around the torso at shoulder height, over the deltoids (like a chest measurement, just higher) — not the tailor's shoulder-tip-to-shoulder-tip line.
  • Chest — around the ribcage at nipple line, relaxed breathing (not inhaled or exhaled).
  • Waist — around your natural waist, the narrowest point, roughly navel level.
  • Hips — around the widest point of your hips/glutes.
  • Arm — around the upper arm/bicep at its widest point, arm relaxed and hanging (not flexed).
  • Thigh — around the upper thigh, just below the glute fold.
  • Calf — around the widest point of the calf muscle, leg straight (not flexed or on tiptoes).

Using Scan or From a photo? You never need a tape at all — just enter your height once. For the best read: full body in frame, arms angled clearly out from your body (elbows away from your ribs, like starting a jumping jack — a small lift isn't enough to separate your arm from your torso in the image) and feet apart, phone roughly at chest height and level (not tilted up/down), fitted clothing.
